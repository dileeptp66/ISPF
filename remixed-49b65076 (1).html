Our Mission and Vision

Vision

Inclusive, Experiential, and Competency Education for Every K-10 Learner

Mission

ISPF delivers accessible, curriculum-aligned, and engaging experiential STEM programmes that lay a strong foundation for every K-10 student with conceptual mastery, real-life connections, and essential competencies, including 21st-century skills and science and maths literacy needed for future careers.

Our Philosophy

We believe that learning is most powerful when it is experiential and curiosity becomes the starting point for discovery and knowledge creation. Our philosophy is rooted in the conviction that science and mathematics education must move beyond textbooks to engage the hands, mind, and heart of every learner.

Our Work

1. Experiential Education

Objective:

To make science and mathematics learning engaging, accessible, and competency-based for K–10 students through experiential, curriculum-aligned STEM programmes.

Approach:

  • Hands-on TACtivities (Tactile Activities) that make abstract concepts tangible
  • Curriculum integration for regular classroom use
  • Continuous monitoring through pre- and post-assessments

2. Teacher Empowerment and Pedagogical Innovation

Objective:

To build teacher capacity in inquiry-based and experiential pedagogy, enabling them to transform classrooms into spaces of discovery.

Approach:

  • Structured teacher training workshops on experiential learning
  • Ongoing mentoring and academic support through the ISPF team
  • Access to digital learning resources in English and regional languages

3. STEM Equity and Inclusion

Objective:

To ensure equitable access to STEM education for all children, particularly girls and students from government schools.

Approach:

  • CSR-funded programmes implemented free of cost to schools
  • Local-language content to improve accessibility
  • Mentorship and exposure opportunities for girl students and underrepresented groups

4. Community and CSR Partnerships

Objective:

To collaborate with CSR foundations, government departments, and NGOs to scale experiential learning and create systemic change.

Approach:

  • Co-designed, outcome-oriented programmes with CSR and government partners
  • Data-driven reporting to demonstrate learning impact
  • Shared ownership with local stakeholders

Raman Award

Celebrating Curiosity. Honouring Innovation.

The Raman Award recognises school students who demonstrate creativity, curiosity, and scientific understanding through hands-on experiments and models that explain real-world science.

Who Can Participate

Open to students from Grades 3–10 in schools across India

What We Celebrate

Originality, creativity, clarity of concept, and practical demonstration

Recognition

Cash awards, free ThinkTac subscription, and mentoring opportunities
